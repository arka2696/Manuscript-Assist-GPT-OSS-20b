Neuroinflammation is increasingly recognized as a key driver of neurodegenerative diseases. 
Recent studies have highlighted the role of microglial activation in clearing beta-amyloid plaques. 
However, chronic activation can lead to synaptic pruning and neuronal death. In our study, we observed a 45% reduction in plaque burden when administering the compound XYZ-123.
Despite this reduction, cognitive tests on the murine models did not show statistically significant improvement after 12 weeks.
